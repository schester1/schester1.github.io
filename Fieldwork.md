<p>
I spent the 25-26 season at Crary Ice Rise in West Antarctica. I was there with the SWAIS2C program, which collected over 200 meters of sediment core from below the grounded ice sheet! While on ice I deployed an ApRES system and helped with a passive and active seismic survey.</p>

During the summer of 2025, I spent four weeks as teaching faculty with the Juneau Icefield Research Program. I led workshops on glacial geomorphology, GIA and sea-level change, and remote sensing with QGIS. During the second half of the course I helped students design and implement their own research project that aimed to constrain isostatic rebound on the icefield.

<p>
I spent two weeks in Arctic Norway coring lakes as part of a project searching for deglacial sea level indicators. Together with Billy D'Andrea, Redmond Stein, and Kristian Vasskog, we used small zodiacs to take gravity cores from three lakes from the modern coastline up to 30 m a.s.l. Investigation of these cores is ongoing, but we hope to better constrain sea-level change over a period of rapid GMSL rise called Meltwater Pulse 1A. This is part of a larger project to determine empirically the source of this meltwater pulse.  </p>

In the Summer of 2023, we travelled to Hawaii to search for paleo sea-level indicators deposited during previous interglacials. We mapped uplifted reef deposits and collected corals for U-series dating. We used tide gauges and GPS surveys to determine the modern tidal range and the elevation of different units.
